The Problem with Churn-First Targeting

Traditional Approach

Rank members by P(churn)

  • Finds the highest-risk members
  • Some of those will churn regardless of outreach ("lost causes")
  • Others would have stayed anyway ("sure things")
  • Wastes budget; may cause over-contact fatigue

Uplift Approach

Rank by ITE = P(churn|no outreach) − P(churn|outreach)

  • Finds members whose behaviour changes because of outreach
  • Skips lost causes and sure things
  • Concentrates budget on "persuadables"
  • Maximises retention per outreach dollar
Key insight: Outreach is expensive. The goal is not to predict who churns — it's to predict who churns because we didn't reach out.

Why confounding matters

Outreach was not random — it was targeted at predicted high-risk members. A naive comparison would understate the true effect. We use propensity weighting to reconstruct a "fair" comparison.

TabPFN + TabICL

State-of-the-art in-context learners for tabular data. No gradient descent at inference — they "read" the training data directly. Strong on small-to-medium datasets with mixed feature types.

Ensemble of 4 variants

Two meta-learners (T-Learner, X-Learner) × two base models (TabPFN, TabICL) = 4 ITE estimates averaged. Reduces variance and model-specific bias.

Recommendations & Next Steps

Immediate actions

  • Target the 2,038 recommended members in the next outreach cycle — ranked by ITE score (predictions.csv)
  • Prioritise the top ~500 members (ITE > 0.10) where confidence is highest
  • Compare realised churn rates of targeted vs untargeted members in 14 days to validate the model

Model limitations to keep in mind

  • ITE estimates rely on conditional ignorability — we assume the 14 features capture all relevant confounding. Unmeasured factors (income, geography, health literacy) could bias estimates.
  • The model cannot distinguish type of outreach — future experiments should test message variants separately.
  • ITE precision is lower at the high-risk tail (few control-group examples there).

Longer-term roadmap

  • Randomised experiment — even a small holdout (5–10% random assignment) will yield ground-truth ITE estimates for model recalibration
  • Continuous retraining — retrain monthly as new behavioral data accumulates
  • Cost-benefit optimisation — once outreach cost per member is known, the ITE threshold can be set to maximise ROI rather than defaulting to ITE > 0
  • Heterogeneous messaging — members with high ITE on diabetes content vs sleep content may respond to different outreach types
Bottom line: Focus outreach on the 2,038 persuadable members. Expected impact: meaningful incremental retention improvement at the same — or lower — outreach budget.
